Digital Humanities in Africa

African Digital Humanities is characterized by a focus on building capacity and infrastructure for digital scholarship, and on promoting access to cultural materials and information for all. African Digital Humanities scholars have been instrumental in the development of digital archives and libraries focused on African culture and history, and in promoting collaboration and networking across the continent.

Archiving and Preservation: This trend is significant due to the rich historical and cultural heritage across the African continent. Digital archiving and preservation efforts help protect these invaluable resources from physical degradation, political instability, or other threats. Moreover, they provide a way for a broader audience to access these historical and cultural artifacts.

Digitization of Indigenous Knowledge: The move towards the digitization and preservation of indigenous knowledge is an important area in Africa, given the continent's cultural and linguistic diversity. By digitizing and preserving indigenous languages, stories, and cultural practices, researchers can help ensure that these aspects of cultural heritage are not lost, while making them accessible to a global audience.

The Network for Digital Humanities in Africa (NDHA) is a vibrant and inclusive platform dedicated to advancing digital humanities across the African continent. Through its online platform and offline activities, the NDHA facilitates knowledge sharing, capacity building, and networking opportunities for individuals and institutions interested in the intersection of digital technologies and the humanities.

The network actively promotes inclusivity and equitable access to digital humanities resources, amplifying underrepresented voices and perspectives to shape the future of African digital scholarship.
